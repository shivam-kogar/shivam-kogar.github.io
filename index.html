Shivam Kogar

I am a undergraduate student at Brown University studying applied mathematics, advised by Professor Chi-Wang Shu. I am most curious about topics involving analysis, probability, and statistics. Some areas I want to learn more about include random polynomials, PDEs, statistical mechanics, perturbation theory, and dynamical systems. I am currently Co-Leader of the Mathematics Department Undergraduate Group at Brown and am passionate about creating inclusive spaces and communities in STEM and mathematics.

My coursework at Brown includes theoretical multivariable calculus, theoretical linear algebra, differential equations, abstract algebra, real analysis, numerical optimization, and introductory and advanced statistics. As part of the Division of Applied Mathematics's Directed Reading Program, I have spent the past year independently studying a mathematical area of interest under the supervision of a graduate student. In Fall 2024, I studied Topics in Random Matrix Theory under the supervision of Jake Mundo, and in Spring 2025 I studied Stochastic Thermodynamics under the supervision of Stanley Nicholson.

In Summer 2025, I am supported by a Undergraduate Teaching & Research Award (UTRA) Fellowship, working with Professor Richard Stratt in the Department of Chemistry at Brown.
